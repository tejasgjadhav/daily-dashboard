🌊 Strait of Hormuz Crisis
Day ~75 of Effective Closure

The US-Iran conflict that erupted on 28 February 2026 — when the US and Israel launched strikes against Iran and assassinated Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei — continues to disrupt global energy markets. Iran's IRGC closed the Strait of Hormuz to Western-allied shipping almost immediately, triggering the largest oil supply shock in history. A fragile ceasefire is technically in place but is under constant strain, with Washington dismissing Tehran's latest peace framework as "totally unacceptable" (Fortune, 11 May).


As of 13 May 2026: A UN Security Council draft resolution co-sponsored by Bahrain and the US and backed by 112 nations — including India, Japan, South Korea and most EU members — demands free navigation through the Strait (Al Jazeera, 13 May). However, China and Russia previously vetoed a similar resolution on 7 April (UK Parliament Library). Traffic through the Strait remains at a near-standstill — only ~40 ships crossed the entire week to 3 May versus a pre-war average of 120 crossings per day (Lloyd's List via CNN). Saudi Aramco CEO Amin Nasser has warned the market is losing ~100 million barrels of supply per week, and market normalization may not occur until 2027 (Trading Economics, 12 May).


For India: India signed on to the 112-nation UN resolution calling for free navigation. Indian-flagged LPG carriers were previously evacuated under Operation Sankalp with Indian Navy escort. Iran has carved out partial passage rights for ships from China, Russia, India, Iraq and Pakistan (Wikipedia / 26 March announcement), but shipping insurance costs remain prohibitive. Continued Hormuz closure = higher imported inflation, weaker rupee, pressure on India's fiscal position and corporate margins.

Latest (13 May, Al Jazeera): 112 nations co-sponsor UN resolution on Hormuz freedom of navigation. Iran's Deputy FM Gharibabadi accuses US of "seeking capitulation rather than peace." Pakistan-mediated talks ongoing. Brent ~$107 despite mild pullback from $110 peak. Trump-Xi meeting in progress; energy discussed alongside trade.
